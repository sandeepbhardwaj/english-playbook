## Overview

Reported speech allows you to communicate what someone said without quoting exact words. When the reporting verb is in the past, tense and time expressions often shift back. Reporting verbs such as say, tell, ask, advise, promise, and suggest also control the grammar that follows.

## Learning Objectives

- Backshift when the reporting context moves into the past.
- Change pronouns and time words to fit the new speaker and moment.
- Choose the reporting verb pattern that matches the message type.

## Key Rules

- Backshift tense when the reporting context is past and the information is not still current.
- Change pronouns, time words, and place words to fit the new perspective.
- Use tell with an object, but say usually appears without one unless say to is used.
- Questions become statements in reported form; use whether/if for yes/no questions and wh-words for wh-questions.
- Different reporting verbs (say, tell, ask, suggest, advise, promise, warn) control the grammar that follows.

## Examples

**Example 1:** "Direct: 'I am tired.' / Reported: She said that she was tired."
*Backshift present to past when reporting context is past.*

**Example 2:** "Direct: 'Do you need help?' / Reported: He asked whether I needed help."
*Questions use statement word order in reported form.*

**Example 3:** "'I will call you,' she promised."
*Reported: She promised to call / She promised that she would call.*

**Example 4:** "Direct: 'Can you help me?' / Reported: She asked if I could help her."
*If/whether for yes/no questions; pronoun and tense both shift.*

**Example 5:** "He said, 'The meeting is at 3 PM tomorrow.' / Reported: He said the meeting was at 3 PM the next day."
*Tomorrow shifts to the next day from the reporter's perspective.*

## Common Mistakes

**Mistake 1:**
- ❌ WRONG: "He asked where was I."
- ✅ RIGHT: "He asked where I was."
- Note: Reported questions use statement word order, not question order.

**Mistake 2:**
- ❌ WRONG: "She said me that she was busy."
- ✅ RIGHT: "She told me that she was busy."
- Note: Say doesn't take an object; tell does.

**Mistake 3:**
- ❌ WRONG: "He said that he is here, yesterday."
- ✅ RIGHT: "He said that he was there yesterday. / He said that he had been here yesterday."
- Note: Here→there, today→that day, yesterday→the day before.

## Quick Reference

Backshift: present→past, past→past perfect, will→would (when reporting into past)
Say + that-clause (no object required); Tell + object + that-clause
Ask + whether/if (yes/no); Ask + why/how/what (wh-questions)
Suggest + that-clause / Suggest + -ing; Advise + object + to-infinitive
Warn + object + not to + verb; Promise + to-infinitive
Pronouns: I→he/she, you→I/he/she, we→they, etc.
Time words: now→then, today→that day, yesterday→the day before, tomorrow→the next day
Place words: here→there, this→that
No backshift if reporting verb is present (He says he is tired = no change)

## Story Lab

### "Story Lab: The Advice"

This narrative uses various reporting verbs and backshift to show how advice and promises flow through a conversation.

"When Arun turned 18, his grandfather called him over. 'I have learned many things in my life,' the grandfather said, 'and I want to share them with you.' He told Arun that success comes from patience and persistence. The old man warned him that shortcuts never lead to lasting achievement."

"Arun asked whether his grandfather had been afraid when starting his own business. The grandfather admitted that he had been terrified but that he had decided to try anyway. He said, 'I am where I am today because I did not give up.' Arun promised that he would remember these words."

"Years later, Arun told his own children what their great-grandfather had told him. He explained that their ancestor had advised him to stay patient. He said that their great-grandfather had also promised him that hard work would eventually pay off. And it had."

**Analysis:**

- Said + that-clause for statements; backshift happens because the reporting verb is in the past.
- Asked whether for yes/no questions; wh-words remain (wondered why, asked how).
- Reporting verbs (told, warned, admitted, promised) vary based on the type of message.

## Summary

Reported speech converts direct words into an indirect narrative. The key shifts are tense (backshift when reporting into the past), pronouns (fit the new speaker), and time words (today becomes that day). Different reporting verbs show different message types.

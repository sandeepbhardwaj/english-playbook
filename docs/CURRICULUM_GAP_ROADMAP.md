# Curriculum Gap Roadmap

This document turns the recent curriculum audit into a practical expansion plan for the next phase of Grammar Atlas.

## Goal

Strengthen topics that are currently only partially covered, without bloating the curriculum or adding a brand-new module too early.

The recommended approach is:

- keep the current 6-module structure
- add 3 new standalone lessons
- split 1 overloaded lesson
- expand the study plan from 12 weeks to 14 weeks

## Audit Summary

### Well Covered

These areas already have clear standalone lesson coverage:

- parts of speech
- sentence structure
- articles, determiners, and nouns
- present forms
- past simple and present perfect
- subject-verb agreement
- pronouns, questions, and negatives
- prepositions and modifiers
- clauses, conjunctions, and relatives
- conditionals and wishes
- reported speech
- punctuation
- parallelism and comparison
- concision and formal tone
- editing, transformations, cloze, and review strategy

### Partially Covered

These are present in the curriculum, but not strongly enough as their own teaching units:

- active and passive voice
- modals in depth
- perfect forms beyond the current bundled treatment
- advanced transformation-style grammar control

### Missing Or Worth A Separate Lesson

These deserve stronger standalone treatment later:

- active and passive voice
- gerunds and infinitives
- verb patterns
- modals in depth
- inversion and emphasis
- causatives
- noun clauses and embedded questions
- advanced determiners and quantifiers

## Recommended Curriculum Changes

### 1. Split One Overloaded Lesson

Current lesson:

- `Future Forms, Perfect Forms, and Modals`

Recommended replacement:

- `Future Forms and Future Time Reference`
- `Modals in Depth`

Why:

- the current lesson covers too many meanings at once
- future reference and modal meaning are related, but not the same teaching problem
- separating them makes quiz design cleaner and learner review easier

### 2. Add A New Lesson In Module 3

Add:

- `Active and Passive Voice`

Best fit:

- Module 3: Sentence Accuracy

Why:

- it is a sentence-level control topic
- learners expect it as a named grammar unit
- it connects naturally to auxiliaries, agreement, editing, and transformations

### 3. Add A New Lesson In Module 4

Add:

- `Gerunds, Infinitives, and Verb Patterns`

Best fit:

- Module 4: Complex Grammar

Why:

- it belongs with more advanced verb-complement and clause-like structures
- it is one of the most common learner problem areas
- it supports reported structures and transformation work later

## Recommended Final Structure

### Module 1: Foundations of Grammar

- Parts of Speech
- Sentence Structure
- Articles, Determiners, and Nouns

### Module 2: Tenses and Time

- Present Simple and Present Continuous
- Past Simple and Present Perfect
- Future Forms and Future Time Reference
- Modals in Depth

### Module 3: Sentence Accuracy

- Subject-Verb Agreement
- Pronouns, Questions, and Negatives
- Prepositions and Modifiers
- Active and Passive Voice

### Module 4: Complex Grammar

- Clauses, Conjunctions, and Relative Clauses
- Conditionals, Wishes, and Hypothetical Meaning
- Reported Speech and Reporting Verbs
- Gerunds, Infinitives, and Verb Patterns

### Module 5: Style and Editing

- Punctuation for Clarity
- Parallelism and Comparison
- Concision and Formal Tone

### Module 6: Mastery and Review

- Error Correction Drills
- Transformations and Cloze Practice
- Final Review and Study Strategy

## Priority Order

Build in this order:

1. Active and Passive Voice
2. Gerunds, Infinitives, and Verb Patterns
3. Modals in Depth
4. Split the current future/modals/perfect lesson into a future-only lesson

Why this order:

- active/passive is the most visible curriculum gap
- gerunds/infinitives is one of the most common learner pain points
- modals already exist partially, so expansion is easier after the first two are done
- future lesson cleanup becomes more natural once modals are separated

## 14-Week Roadmap

This is the recommended pacing after the curriculum upgrade.

### Week 1

- Theme: Word jobs
- Study Parts of Speech
- Label words in ten original sentences
- Take the lesson quiz and review explanations

### Week 2

- Theme: Sentence core
- Study Sentence Structure
- Study Articles, Determiners, and Nouns
- Repair fragments and article mistakes in one paragraph

### Week 3

- Theme: Present meaning
- Study Present Simple and Present Continuous
- Identify tense choices in the story lesson
- Complete the lesson quiz and review errors

### Week 4

- Theme: Past meaning and present relevance
- Study Past Simple and Present Perfect
- Write a short reflection using both forms
- Retake the quiz if the score stays weak

### Week 5

- Theme: Future reference
- Study Future Forms and Future Time Reference
- Compare will, going to, present continuous, and future perfect
- Write a short plan or prediction paragraph

### Week 6

- Theme: Modal meaning
- Study Modals in Depth
- Practice advice, obligation, deduction, permission, and probability
- Complete contrast drills on must, should, may, might, can, and have to

### Week 7

- Theme: Agreement and reference
- Study Subject-Verb Agreement
- Study Pronouns, Questions, and Negatives
- Edit one older paragraph only for these targets

### Week 8

- Theme: Pattern accuracy
- Study Prepositions and Modifiers
- Build a phrase notebook from reading examples
- Complete one timed editing drill

### Week 9

- Theme: Voice and control
- Study Active and Passive Voice
- Convert active to passive and passive to active in mixed tenses
- Retake a weak lesson quiz and log mistakes in the error log

### Week 10

- Theme: Complex links
- Study Clauses, Conjunctions, and Relative Clauses
- Combine simple sentences into more complex ones
- Review punctuation at clause boundaries

### Week 11

- Theme: Hypothetical and reported meaning
- Study Conditionals, Wishes, and Hypothetical Meaning
- Study Reported Speech and Reporting Verbs
- Retell a conversation indirectly in writing or sentence transformation form

### Week 12

- Theme: Verb patterns
- Study Gerunds, Infinitives, and Verb Patterns
- Practice verb + infinitive, verb + gerund, and object + infinitive patterns
- Complete transformation and correction drills

### Week 13

- Theme: Clarity in editing
- Study Punctuation for Clarity
- Study Parallelism and Comparison
- Revise a formal paragraph for logic and flow

### Week 14

- Theme: Tone and mastery
- Study Concision and Formal Tone
- Study Error Correction Drills
- Study Transformations and Cloze Practice
- Finish with Final Review and Study Strategy

## Lesson Creation Rules

Every new lesson should include:

- clear focus statement
- summary
- objectives
- rules
- examples
- pitfalls
- practice plan
- story or context block
- revision summary
- cheat sheet
- quiz coverage notes

This keeps the new lessons aligned with the existing Grammar Atlas lesson architecture.

## Not Recommended Right Now

Do not add these yet unless the platform becomes more exam-focused:

- inversion and emphasis
- causatives
- noun clauses and embedded questions
- advanced determiners and quantifiers
- reduced relative clauses

These are good later, but not as urgent as the four roadmap changes above.

## Best Next Build Sequence

Recommended implementation phases:

### Phase 1

- add `Active and Passive Voice`
- add `Gerunds, Infinitives, and Verb Patterns`

### Phase 2

- split `Future Forms, Perfect Forms, and Modals`
- create `Future Forms and Future Time Reference`
- create `Modals in Depth`

### Phase 3

- revise the roadmap from 12 weeks to 14 weeks
- rebalance quiz coverage and recommendation logic if needed

### Phase 4

- consider advanced elective lessons only after the core gaps are fully stable

## Recommendation

The best next version of the curriculum is:

- no new module
- 3 new lessons
- 1 lesson split into 2 cleaner lessons
- roadmap expansion from 12 weeks to 14 weeks

That gives Grammar Atlas stronger topic completeness while keeping the product simple, teachable, and maintainable.

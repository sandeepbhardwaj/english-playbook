## Overview

Some verbs are followed by gerunds, some by infinitives, and some by either form with a change of meaning. This is one of the most common learner pain points because the choice often cannot be guessed from logic alone. Strong control comes from learning patterns together with meaning: enjoy doing, decide to do, stop doing, stop to do, remember doing, remember to do, and similar contrasts.

## Learning Objectives

- Recognize common verb + gerund, verb + infinitive, and verb + object + infinitive patterns.
- Explain meaning differences in pairs such as stop doing vs. stop to do.
- Use these patterns accurately in realistic speaking and writing contexts.

## Key Rules

- A gerund is the -ing form used as a noun-like structure; an infinitive is to + base verb.
- Some verbs are regularly followed by gerunds (enjoy, avoid, consider, suggest).
- Some verbs are regularly followed by infinitives (decide, hope, plan, refuse, want).
- Some verbs can take either form, but the meaning may change (remember, forget, stop, try).
- Some patterns require an object before the infinitive (advise someone to..., want someone to..., allow someone to...).

## Examples

**Example 1:** "She enjoys reading before bed."
*Enjoy is followed by a gerund.*

**Example 2:** "We decided to leave early."
*Decide is followed by an infinitive.*

**Example 3:** "He avoided answering the question directly."
*Avoid takes a gerund.*

**Example 4:** "I forgot to lock the door."
*Forgot to lock = the action did not happen.*

**Example 5:** "I'll never forget meeting her for the first time."
*Forget meeting = memory of a past event.*

**Example 6:** "They stopped talking when the presentation began."
*Stop talking = end the activity.*

**Example 7:** "They stopped to talk to the presenter."
*Stop to talk = pause one action in order to do another.*

**Example 8:** "The teacher encouraged us to revise the final paragraph."
*Object + infinitive pattern.*

## Common Mistakes

**Mistake 1:**
- ❌ WRONG: "I enjoy to read at night."
- ✅ RIGHT: "I enjoy reading at night."
- Note: Enjoy takes a gerund, not an infinitive.

**Mistake 2:**
- ❌ WRONG: "She suggested to leave earlier."
- ✅ RIGHT: "She suggested leaving earlier. / She suggested that we leave earlier."
- Note: Suggest does not normally take to-infinitive.

**Mistake 3:**
- ❌ WRONG: "I remembered locking the door, so I went back to do it."
- ✅ RIGHT: "I remembered to lock the door, so I didn't need to go back."
- Note: Remember doing = memory; remember to do = remembered an obligation.

## Quick Reference

Common gerund verbs: enjoy, avoid, consider, suggest, keep, finish, mind
Common infinitive verbs: decide, hope, plan, want, need, refuse, promise
Object + infinitive: ask someone to, advise someone to, want someone to, allow someone to
No to after let / make: let me go, made us wait
Remember doing = memory of past event
Remember to do = remember obligation before action
Stop doing = cease activity
Stop to do = pause one action in order to do another
Try doing = experiment with a method
Try to do = make an effort

## Story Lab

### "Story Lab: Choosing the Right Internship"

This story shows how verb patterns carry meaning and why small form changes can change the whole message.

"Riya hoped to find an internship that would challenge her, but she also wanted to avoid wasting a summer on routine tasks. She enjoyed solving practical problems, and she imagined working with a team that encouraged her to ask difficult questions."

"At one interview, the manager suggested joining the analytics group. Later, Riya remembered meeting one of the team leads at a conference, and she remembered to send him a follow-up note. When another company delayed its offer, she stopped checking her email every ten minutes and tried focusing on her portfolio instead."

"In the end, she chose the role that made her feel excited to learn. She never regretted accepting it. The work pushed her to think carefully, and it taught her that understanding verb patterns in English is a little like understanding career choices: small differences in form can produce big differences in meaning."

**Analysis:**

- The story contrasts gerund-following verbs such as enjoy and avoid with infinitive-following verbs such as hope and want.
- Remember meeting and remembered to send show the classic memory-vs.-obligation contrast.
- Stop checking and tried focusing show how verb-pattern choices change nuance, not just grammar form.

## Summary

Verb patterns must often be learned as complete units. Some verbs take gerunds, some take infinitives, some take object + infinitive, and some allow both with different meanings. Focus on high-frequency contrasts such as remember, forget, stop, try, regret, advise, let, and make.
